Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

1. Introduction

CharityConnect ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your personal data. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Irish Data Protection Act 2018.

2. Data Controller

The data controller for CharityConnect is Robbie Wiseman. If you have questions about how your data is handled, you can contact us at robbie.wiseman13@gmail.com.

3. What Data We Collect

We collect the following personal data when you use CharityConnect:

  • Account information: Full name, email address, and hashed password when you register.
  • Organiser details: Organisation name, charity number, and contact email if you register as an organiser.
  • Order and payment data: Purchase details, ticket quantities, donation amounts, and Stripe payment references. Card details are processed directly by Stripe and are never stored on our servers.
  • Consent records: Timestamps of when you agreed to these terms and any marketing consent preferences.
  • Audit data: Records of key actions (purchases, verification decisions, refund requests) for platform integrity and traceability.

4. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • Account management: To create and maintain your account, authenticate logins, and manage roles.
  • Ticket processing: To process purchases, generate receipts, and send order confirmations via email.
  • Refund handling: To process and communicate refund request decisions.
  • Verification: To review organiser and charity applications submitted through the platform.
  • Platform analytics: To generate aggregated, non-identifying statistics such as total tickets sold and funds raised.
  • Communication: To send transactional emails (receipts, refund decisions, impact summaries). Marketing emails are only sent if you have given explicit consent.

5. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your data on the following legal bases under GDPR:

  • Contract (Article 6(1)(b)): Processing necessary to fulfil ticket purchases and provide platform services.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a)): For marketing communications and acceptance of these terms at registration.
  • Legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f)): For audit logging, fraud prevention, and platform security.

6. Third-Party Services

CharityConnect uses the following third-party services that may process your data:

  • Stripe: For secure payment processing. Stripe's privacy policy is available at stripe.com/privacy.
  • Brevo (Sendinblue): For transactional email delivery in production. Brevo's privacy policy is available at brevo.com/legal/privacypolicy.
  • Neon: For cloud-hosted PostgreSQL database services.
  • Render: For application hosting.
  • OpenRouter / Meta Llama: For optional AI-generated event descriptions and marketing suggestions. No personal user data is sent to the AI service.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active. Order and payment records are retained for audit and compliance purposes. If you delete your account, your personal data will be removed, though anonymised audit records may be retained for platform integrity.

8. Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate data via your Account Settings page.
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten").
  • Withdraw consent: Withdraw marketing consent at any time via Account Settings without affecting prior processing.
  • Data portability: Request your data in a commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Restriction: Request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Objection: Object to processing based on legitimate interest.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at robbie.wiseman13@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days as required by GDPR.

9. Cookies

CharityConnect uses session cookies to keep you logged in and to protect against cross-site request forgery (CSRF). These are essential cookies required for the platform to function and do not track you across other websites. We do not use advertising or analytics cookies.

10. Data Security

We take reasonable measures to protect your data, including: password hashing (Werkzeug/bcrypt), CSRF protection on all forms, HTTPS encryption in production, and secure environment variable management for API keys and database credentials.

11. Children's Privacy

CharityConnect is not intended for users under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If we become aware that a child has provided us with personal data, we will take steps to delete it.

12. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date. Continued use of CharityConnect after changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.

13. Contact and Complaints

For questions about this policy or to exercise your data rights, contact robbie.wiseman13@gmail.com.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Irish Data Protection Commission:
www.dataprotection.ie
